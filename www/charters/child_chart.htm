CHILDREN’S CHARTER ON ELECTRONIC MEDIA

• We, the junior delegates of the Summit, wish to thank the Summit for the invitation and for the opportunity to express our opinions about children’s television around the world. We have tried to be fair and honest in our comments, and have some constructive criticism.

• We represent children from all over the world and of all ages.

• We think some people on television don’t seem to like children.

• We feel our opinions aren’t always respected. No one consults us about what we want or what we need.

• Many of the programs made for children don’t allow children to express themselves.

• Some of the programs contain a lot of violence, or rather, violence for the sake of violence. We like action, lots of it, but that doesn’t mean programs have to be violent. Violence to solve conflict should not be promoted on television.

• Some programs exploit children. They are made only to sell toys and other products for children.

• We want all children to be able to see people like themselves on television. Why can’t children wear glasses on television? Why can’t some children on television be fat? A lot of children all over the world have never seen someone like them on television, speaking their own language. Sometimes, all they watch are American programs.

• To help change all this, we have written a Children’s Charter on Electronic Media.
